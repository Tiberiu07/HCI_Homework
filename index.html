Abstract

The Bachelor Thesis project is a very important academic endeavour for every student. Therefore, it goes without saying that the user experience has to be taken into consideration while developing the application. A usable and intuitive user interface I would consider to be the bare-minimum standard for such a complex project. Throughout this article, I will discuss different HCI principles, methods and best practices applied to my Bachelor Thesis software application - InvestoBuddy.

Introduction

Human Computer Interaction comes from a book in the 80s that was about the psychology of someone interacting with a machine. It is all about the aspect of how a person feels when using a particular software product. It does NOT come down only to making things simpler and more intuitive. There is the aspect of graphical design that also comes into play. For example, this may be the types of images used to affect people's psychology. As a practical example: some colors may work better for different purposes (for example some are more persuasive or other are more powerful in ways that they create more urgency etc.). Also, typgraphy, sounds, aesthetics could be considered important elements that can effect the human experience. Those who studied and worked in HCI saw it as a crucial instrument to popularize the idea that the interaction between a computer and the user should resemble a human-to-human, open-ended dialogue. “…it no longer makes sense to regard HCI as a specialty of computer science; HCI has grown to be broader, larger and much more diverse than computer science itself“

Throughout this short article, I will cover some important subject areas of Human Computer Interaction, briefly explaining what each and every area is concerned about and give some practical examples on how some of these areas have been / or not applied through my InvestoBuddy project. The covered areas will be the following:

  • Human Factors
  • Design Models, Methodologies and Guidelines.
  • Information Architecture. Visual Design.
  • Design Patterns for Social Interactions.

Human Factors

When we are talking about human factors, it is mandatory to mention that currently software products are described by multiple interaction idioms, meaning that users are accustomed to some specific types/styles of user interfaces. Each particular style has its own vocabulary of items, actions, sensorial (e.g., visual, auditory) representations. Examples: forms, text editors, web browsers, multimedia players etc. Also, besides different types of user interfaces we have to consider that software applicatiosn are also used in different contexts: entertainment & leisure software, media players, social applications, games, smart appliances/home. We have to take all these aspects into consideration when designing an application. The first question we have to give ourselves would be: who is the user who will use it? And when we have a prompt answer to this question we have to adapt the mentioned factors to our targeted audience. We have to answer to questions like : who is our primary targeted user? But our secondary targeted user? Tertiary users etc.

When it comes down to the application which I have designed (InvestoBuddy), I tried to take all the mentioned factors into consideration. I used familar elements as components of my UI. For example I used typical, intuitive login/register forms or helpful Sidebar to assure that the user experience is at the upmost level of quality. Every decision regarding the UI was taken with the question "How can I enhance the user experience?" in mind. From big, reachable buttons to searchbars and sidebars, I have used all the mentioned factors (items, actions, sensorial representations) to my advantage. My targeted primary user is a highly financially educated person who is interested in areas like bussiness, trading and the stock prices situation as a whole. It is a person who is an adept of continous improvement and education. The application is intuitive because it was developed in a manner to resemble some other familiar financial platforms.

Design Models, Methodologies and Guidelines.

A successful product is a balanced combination of several factors, like Desirability, Viability and Capacity. All these have to be taken into consideration when developing and designing a software product. While Software Engineering is concerned with the communication between application and developers or between teams of developers, Human Computer Interaction is mainly focused on the communication with the user. Interface design requires an important development effort. A task-centered design, focused ONLY on the developer's goals can ruin a whole project. At the other end of the spectrum developing an app that takes into consideration ONLY user needs and almost never a developer's concern is also a recipe for disaster. This is why making the right choices regarding these aspects is nothing less and nothing more than a well-balanced dance, where all the elements interact flawlessly one with another.

If we are talking about InvestoBuddy design, I have tried to balance all the factors very well. My first and foremost concern was the user experience, but I have also considered the ease of implementation when taking particular decisions regarding using certain components in the design. For example, when designing the sidebar I wanted in the first version of the design to add a particular animation to the header text to enhance the user visual experience. But from an implementation perspective, I came to the conclusion that this would be a pretty bad idea, because it is hard to implement an animation, but add little value to the project. In contrast, I never wanted to add a search bar for the stocks, but I eventually came to the conclusion that this would be an important element that will enhance the user experience. This is why I consider that developing a good and useful software product is a well-balanced dance.

Information Architecture

The essential problem that designers are dealing with is that information should be properly structured and presented in order to be easily perceived and consumed by users. Information architecture is the practice of deciding how to arrange the parts of something to be understandable. This is achieved through using hierarchies, categorizations, navigation, and metadata for a (digital) product such as an application or Web site.

In my InvestoBuddy bachelor thesis application, the information architecture aspect was taken as seriously as possible. It is essential for a user to be able to visualise stock price information data in a clear and coherent way. Because when we are talking about financial aspects, each details matters. This is why besides the market price for a particular stock I have included data such as a graph that presents the evolution of the price during different time intervals, such as 1 day, 1 week, 1 month etc. I took all the measures to present the stocks data in a clear way. And I believe this is an important step towards developing a quality software application because a proper information architecture enhances the user experience.

Design Patterns for Social Interactions.

Microinteractions are also a details to be taken into consideration when developing a software product. Because each detail matters when it comes to user experience. And tiny details like changing the color when a password becomes valid really can make the difference. I will mention some of the most important design patterns concerning web and mobile applications: Safe Exploration (Let me explore without getting lost or getting into trouble), Instant gratification (I want to accomplish something now, not later), Satisficing (This is good enough. I don’t want to spend more time learning to do it better). Changes in midstream (I changed my mind about what I was doing), Deferred choices (I don’t want to answer that now; just let me finish), Incremental Construction (Let me change this. That doesn’t look right; let me change it again. That’s better!) and others..

When I designed InvestoBuddy, I took into consideration also some popular design patterns. For example, when it comes to Safe Exploration, I implemented a SideBar that lets the user easily navigate between application screens without getting lost or getting into trouble. Also, with this design pattern in mind, I've implemented the SearchBar to facilitate the process of finding particular stock actions. If we are talking about Instant Gratification, I instantly provide feedback when a user enters his login credentials. (success or failure). If we are talking about the Satisficing design pattern, I implemented the "Instrumente Financiare" page in such a way that if a user does not need any other information than the stock price and graph financial details, he does not have to scroll all the way down for more detailed info (which is still easily available). Incremental Construction -> the user has the possibility of incrementally building his list of following companies in a way that he desires. Habituation -> the user has similar interactive buttons throughout all the application etc. These are the main design patterns used throughout my application.

QOC Method Examples

Question: How a user could access more easily the stocks information withould scrolling through all the available financial instruments?
Answer: scrollist, searchbar, menu of categories, combobox, various selectors
Criteria: Efficiency, Pleasant User Experience, Speed
Chosen Solution: Scrollist + Searchbar

Question: How a user can visualise stock market data?
Answer: Pie Charts, Histograms, Simple Liniar Graphs
Criteria: Pleasant Data Visualisation, Clarity
Chosen Solution: Simple Liniar Graphs

Question: How a user can move through pages?
Answer: Hamburger Menu, Foldable Menu, Fixed Sidebar
Criteria: Design, Simplicity of Use
Chosen Solution: Fixed Sidebar

Personas Document

Type: Primary
Detailed Description: A highly financially educated person interested in staying in touch with everything what is happening on the stock market. Wants to update his knowledge regarding financial topics and keep himself informed with the latest news and trends regarding the price trends. Someone who is always looking to improve and become better each day.
Main Points: Highly financially educated, intelligent, smart, willing to improve
Goals: Personal and bussiness goals. To become more educated. To improve.
Frustrations and Pain Points: Details oriented, interested in correct and updated information only.
Scenarios: The person comes from work and opens the application to check the financial situation. Another scenario would be that the person stays in a traffic jam and wantes to spend his time proactively by using InvestoBuddy.

Type: Secondary
Detailed Description: A regular curious person who wants to find out new things and takes into consideration financial education. Someone who does not have particular interest for the stock market, but is curious to understand how is it working and how other people make money ouf of it. Someone who wants to check some financial news in order to expand his general culture.
Main Points: Very curious persona who likes to find out about new things. Interested in learning and improving. Wants to expand his horizons.
Goals: Horizons Expansion. Improving general culture.
Frustrations and Pain Points: Does not understand some of the complex and minutious financial details or how the stock market works overall.
Scenarios: This particular person will land on the website and check the financial news to expand his horizons in the financial world. Also he will check the informative finacial instruments articles.

Type: Tertiary
Detailed Description: Someone who landed on the web page mostly by mistake or from their friend's advice but they are actually not particularly interested in developing their financial education. Usually this person is really bad regarding finance management and lives paycheck to paycheck and does not care at all about investing in any financial products whatsoever
Main Points: Financially Disinterested. Careless regarding his financial future. Doesn't invest at all.
Goals: Use his time so that it can pass faster
Frustrations and Pain Points: Does not understand anything about financial and what this website is about.
Scenarios: This person has good intentions. Some of his relatives gave him the advice to become more interested in this area because the financial world is very important. Tries to use his time in a "productive" manner, but due to his lack of interest he ends up just scrolling around.

Type: Negative
Detailed Description: Advanced technical person with ill-intentions, who wants to destabilise the website and generate damages. Not interested in finances whatsoever. Just wants to harm the website. In other words, the ill-intentioned hacker.
Main Points: Ill-intentioned hacker who wants to destabilise the website and stop it from running. Hacker.
Goals: To take the website offline and generate financial damages.
Frustrations and Pain Points: Psychological disorders. Childhood traumas that determined him to pick the bad side.
Scenarios: He will try to generate DDoS attacks. The website will require authentication and limit the number of requests per second to combat these types of attack. He will try to spam the server with requests. Or maybe try to exploit some bugs of the application. The developer team will respond promptly regarding these kind of situations.
